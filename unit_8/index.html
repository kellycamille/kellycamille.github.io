Who We Are

G.I.R.L., Inc. is a Washington, D.C. based nonprofit organization. Our organization focuses on girls ages 10-16 whose parent(s) are/were incarcerated and/or absent. We strive to empower young girls to see their self-worth while developing skills for the real world.

What We Do

  • Decrease emotional/social isolation from families and friends by providing a nurturing environment for growth and development.
  • Mentor through one-on-one support groups.
  • Build positive/healthy self esteem.
  • Arrange prision visitation to incarcerated parent(s). Break the potential cycle of incarceration passed from parent-to-child.
  • Network with organizations with similar vision.
  • Provide educational support through tutoring and referrals to resource agencies.
  • Promote community service.

G.I.R.L., Inc. Workshops

  1. Elephant in the Room (Support Group and One-on-One Mentoring) - We will discuss their feelings toward their parents being incarcerated and other personal situations the girls would like to dicuss. The young girls will always keep a journal to write their thoughts down.
  2. Bridging the Gap - Some girls will take monthly visits to see their parent(s) in prison with the approval of their guardian. When parents are released from prison, we will provide one-on-one support for the parent and young girl to bride the gap of their relationship; to strenghten and to make it grow.
  3. Mirror, Mirror - Girls will learn to appreciate their selves in a positive way. We will explore healthy eating choices, having a pure spirit (spirtual guidance) and having an active lifestyle (exercising and dancing)
  4. My Body, My Home - Girls will focus on health concerns for teen adolescents and motivate the girls on how to choose healthy behaviors.
  5. Girl Power - Young girls will learn how to live smart. They will learn how to manage money, invest and how to run a business. Girls will also learn to develop leadership skills.
  6. A Day of Service - Girls will be engaged in community service once a month. Girls will learn how to give back to their community.
  7. Education First - G.I.R.L., Inc. belives that education comes first. We will tutor young girls in various school subjects and assist juniors and seniors with college preparation (i.e. applications and scholarships).
  8. Im a Lady - An etiquette class will be administered to teach manners.

Why Mentor

National studies have shown that mentoring programs decrease:

  • Education Deficits - 98% of youths paired with mentors stayed in school
  • Illegal Drug Use - 85% of youths who had a mentor did not use drugs
  • Teen Pregnancy - 98% of teens matched with mentors avoid teen preganancy
